Dragonfly Landing

Instead of going the AirBnb, Vrbo route a couple wanted their own dedicated website to feature their short-term rental cottage in Maine.

Our goal was a stylish, lean-and-clean site that was set up to add increased functionality (blog posts, direct booking, etc) over time.

www.dragonflylandingmaine.com

Challenges

The function and layout of booking sites have become second nature to the public. So how do we incorporate the "familiar" with a more unique design that works regardless of screen size and is not bloated with content?

We started with an arial picture that grabs your attention. Next the image carousel cycles through a variety of shots at a steady pace. The "stats bar" shows quick-glance, high-level infomation about the cottage and the familiar "tiles" showing details of each room, finally followed by a scroll-able picture gallery.

The end result is a unique, information rich and expandable site that stands out against template generated counterparts.

Productivity & Income Tracking App

Audiobook narrarators, as with many freelancers, find a challange in effectivly measuring productivity for their projects. Tracking royalities across numerous titles, publishers and platforms can also create a burden of work that always gets moved to the bottom of the to-do list.

Other productivity or accounting apps try to be all things to all industries. What if there was one web based app that was targeted specifically for Audiobook narrators that helped track your hours and income based on simple inputs?

Challenges

The idea of moving a "highly functional spreadsheet" into an app sounds like it may be simple but you quickly find that an abundance of required inputs drags system performance and is difficult to navigate on smaller screens.

Aside from UI/UX components the functionality is fairly straight foward. The greater challenge is appropriately storing, securing and retrieving user data.

Watch this space for future updates.
